Juan Ponce Enrile cleared in all pork barrel cases

Oct 24, 2025 10:15 AM PHT
Jairo Bolledo

SUMMARY

This is AI generated summarization, which may have errors. For context, always refer to the full article.

HEARING. Chief Presidential Legal Counsel Juan Ponce Enrile attends the public hearing on a proposal to amend or revise the 1987 Constitution at the Senate on March 22, 2023.

Rappler

Sandiganbayan acquits Enrile in his graft cases, clearing him both in his plunder and graft cases related to the pork barrel scam

MANILA, Philippines – The anti-graft court Sandiganbayan acquitted Chief Presidential Legal Counsel Juan Ponce Enrile of graft on Friday, October 24, clearing him in all of his pork barrel scam cases.

Enrile’s win means it’s still a 0 win, 3 losses for the Benigno Aquino III government’s biggest pork barrel scam cases.

The 101-year-old former senator scored his plunder acquittal last year, while Senator Jinggoy Estrada and former senator Bong Revilla had also won in their plunder cases.

The promulgation was originally set on September 12, but the anti-graft court moved it to a later date. The Special Third Division then said that the decision required “further deliberation,” and one of the division members handling the cases submitted a “reflection” paper.

Of the three, only Enrile was not jailed (only hospital arrest) after he was granted bail by the Supreme Court. The controversial ruling on humanitarian bail, penned by former chief justice and now Executive Secretary Lucas Bersamin, introduced a “new doctrine” in provisional liberty, which also helped President Ferdinand Marcos Jr.’s mother Imelda avoid detention after her 2018 graft conviction.

Like Estrada and Revilla, Enrile faced plunder and graft cases due to their alleged Priority Development Assistance Fund-related transactions with Janet Lim Napoles.

Both Enrile and Revilla are now cleared of their plunder and graft cases, while Estrada has remaining graft cases.

Estrada was absolved by the Sandiganbayan in his plunder case, but convicted him of direct and indirect bribery. Later, the anti-graft court granted his appeal in the conviction, clearing him of the case.

Enrile’s graft cases ranked among the high-profile cases that moved slowest, with trial starting in 2019. — Rappler.com
